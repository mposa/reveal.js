A (Controlled) Hybrid Systems View

Example: $u(x)$ are GRF, $G(x)$ governs step-timing, and $s(x)$ is step location

Polcy: controller $u(x)$, hybrid guard $G(x)$,
and discrete controller $s(x)$ where

  • Execute $u(x)$ whenever $G(x) > 0$
  • Take a step with discrete input $s(x)$ whenever $G(x) = 0$

Lyapunov Analysis

\begin{align*} G(x) > 0 &\Rightarrow \dot V(x,u(x)) \leq 0 \\ G(x) = 0 &\Rightarrow V(R(x,s(x))) \leq V(x) \end{align*}
But $G,u,s$ are all unknown, so this is non-convex$^*$

$N$-step Capture Regions1

Recursively find policy states that can be stabilized in at most $N$ steps.

Occupation Measures Approach2

Convex formulation of outer approximation to reachable set

\begin{align*} \min_{V(t,x),W(x)} \qquad &\int_{\mathcal{X}}W(x)dx \\ \text{subject to}\\ x \in \mathcal{X}_{goal}, t \in \mathcal{T} &\Rightarrow V(t,x) \geq 0 \\ x \in \mathcal{X}, u \in \mathcal{U}, t \in \mathcal{T} &\Rightarrow -\dot V(t,x) \geq 0 \\ x \in \mathcal{X} &\Rightarrow W(x) \geq 0 \\ x \in \mathcal{X}, t \in \mathcal{T} & \Rightarrow W(x) - V(t,x) - 1 \geq 0 \end{align*}

The 1-sublevel set of $V(t,x)$ provably cannot reach the goal set

2Henrion and Korda. Convex computation of the region of attraction of polynomial control systems. TAC, 2014.

$N$-step Reachability

Solve for $V_N(t,x)$ and $W_N(x)$ using $(N-1)$-step solution by choosing \begin{equation}\mathcal{X}_{goal} = \{x \in \mathcal{X} : V_{N-1}(0,R(x,s)) \geq 0 \}\end{equation}

\begin{align*} \min_{V_N(t,x),W_N(x)} \qquad &\int_{\mathcal{X}}W_N(x)dx \\ \text{subject to}\\ x \in \mathcal{X}_{goal}, t \in \color{highlight}\mathcal{T}_{step} &\Rightarrow V_N(t,x) \geq 0 \\ x \in \mathcal{X}, u \in \mathcal{U}, t \in \mathcal{T} &\Rightarrow -\dot V_N(t,x) \geq 0 \\ x \in \mathcal{X} &\Rightarrow W_N(x) \geq 0 \\ x \in \mathcal{X}, t \in \mathcal{T} & \Rightarrow W_N(x) - V_N(t,x) - 1 \geq 0 \end{align*}
Approximates set of states that can reach $(N-1)$-step capture region with some inputs $u(t)$ and $s$ in time $t \in \mathcal{T}_{step}$

Modeling: Centroidal Dynamics

Relax constant height and angular momentum assumption

\begin{align*} q = \begin{bmatrix} x_{cm} \\ z_{cm} \\ \theta \end{bmatrix}, \qquad \ddot q = \begin{bmatrix} \frac{f_x}{m} \\ \frac{f_z}{m} - g \\ \frac{1}{I}\left(f_zx_{cm} - f_xz_{cm} \right)\end{bmatrix}\\ \end{align*}
